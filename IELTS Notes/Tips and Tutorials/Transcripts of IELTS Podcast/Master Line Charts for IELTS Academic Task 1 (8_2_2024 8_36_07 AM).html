10 Dynamic Verbs with Adverbs

For an imagined line chart detailing the rise in international students in the UK, here are some verbs and adverbs to paint a clear and compelling picture:

  1. Growing steadily: “The chart shows that the enrolments of foreign students in 2022 were growing steadily.”
  2. Falling dramatically: “In contrast, 2023 saw the enrolment figures falling dramatically.”
  3. Consistently improving: “The University of London, however, witnessed a consistently improving trend.”
  4. Rapidly increasing: “The number of EU students was rapidly increasing.”
  5. Gradually declining: “Non-EU enrolments were gradually declining.”
  6. Slightly fluctuating: “Engineering programmes showed a slightly fluctuating pattern.”
  7. Sharply rising: “Art courses, on the other hand, had enrolment figures sharply rising.”
  8. Plummeting: “Masters programmes experienced their popularity plummeting.”
  9. Steadily: “The number of doctoral candidates was soaring.”
  10. Surging: “The influx of Australian students was surging.”

The final three verbs are strong enough on their own, and do need an adverb. 

10 Different Transitional Devices

Transitional devices are the magic carpet of essay writing. They gracefully carry your reader from one idea to the next. Here are some you can use in your line chart descriptions:

  1. However
  2. Furthermore
  3. Consequently
  4. On the other hand
  5. Meanwhile
  6. In contrast
  7. Therefore
  8. Simultaneously
  9. Nonetheless
  10. In comparison

Comparisons: Data Point Delineation

When comparing different data points on the chart, the right conjunctions can make all the difference. Try these five:

  1. While: “While the number of EU students increased, non-EU enrolments dropped.”
  2. Whereas: “Whereas enrolments for Art courses shot up, those for Engineering showed no significant change.”
  3. Although: “Although Indian student numbers surged, the numbers from China remained steady.”
  4. Despite: “Despite a fall in 2023, the enrolments rebounded the following year.”
  5. On the contrary: “On the contrary to the decreasing trend in Masters programmes, doctoral enrolments were on the rise.”

5 Tips to Improve Your Graph Description Skills

  1. Don’t describe every tiny change. Instead, focus on the overall trends and significant data points.
  2. Remember to provide an overview or summary of the main trends you see in the chart.
  3. Use a variety of grammatical structures and vocabularies. Don’t repeat the same phrase too often.
  4. Always maintain a formal tone. This is an academic essay, after all!
  5. Time management is key. Don’t spend too much time on Task 1. You should spend about 20 minutes on this task, leaving 40 minutes for Task 2.
